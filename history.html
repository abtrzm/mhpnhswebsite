School's History

Founding in Baliuag
1903-1905

Established as Bulacan (Provincial) High School, it started humbly in a rented house in Baliuag. With a mission to attract students from Spanish-era colegios, it gradually expanded its curriculum, offering full high school courses by 1906.

The Move to Malolos
1906

A new chapter began when the school relocated to Malolos, with a brand-new building in the provincial capitol area. By June 1906, students settled into their new home, marking the school’s official rise as a premier learning institution in Bulacan.

The First Graduates
1909

The pioneering batch of five students—Juan Sepulveda Fernando, Gabriel Adriano Bernardo, Miguela Guevara Baltazar, Enrique Florencio Pangilinan Ochoa, and Irene San Juan—paved the way for future generations, with most pursuing higher education at the newly established University of the Philippines.

The 1912 Student Strike
1912

A bold stand for students' rights, the 1912 strike erupted in protest against the strict and allegedly abusive Principal William E. McVey. Despite facing severe consequences, the students' courage became a symbol of activism and justice in Philippine education.

Surviving World War II
1940

As war ravaged the country, many students took up arms and joined the guerrilla resistance. Some, like Cecilio Abraham and Genaro Bernabe, paid the ultimate price for their bravery, forever etching their names in history.

Renaming to Marcelo H. del Pilar High School
1951

In honor of the great Bulakenyo hero, the school was officially renamed through Republic Act 618, ensuring that future generations would carry the spirit of Marcelo H. del Pilar’s patriotism and courage.

Relocation to Sta. Isabel, Malolos
1984

A major milestone was reached when the school moved to a sprawling 10-hectare campus in Sta. Isabel. Inaugurated on July 31, 1984, the new site provided a modern and spacious environment for students to thrive.

Becoming a National High School
2003

Reflecting its growing prestige, the institution was officially renamed Marcelo H. del Pilar National High School, solidifying its status as one of the country’s most respected educational institutions.
